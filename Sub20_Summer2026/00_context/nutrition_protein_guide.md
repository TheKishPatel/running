## Lunch (~40g) — Priority Focus

This is where the target is most likely to fall short. Aim for a protein-forward main + one supplement.

### Pret A Manger

| Item | Protein | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| Chicken Caesar wrap | ~30g | Best single-item option |
| Chicken & Avocado sandwich | ~28g | |
| Tuna crunch baguette | ~28g | |
| Smoked salmon & egg wrap | ~25g | |
| Hot chicken noodle soup | ~20g | Too low on its own |

**Supplement if main is under 30g:**
- Protein pot (eggs + edamame) → +10–12g
- Large whole milk flat white → +8g (and counts toward carbs too)
- Yoghurt pot → +6–8g

**Simple rule:** Chicken or fish main + one protein add-on = 35–42g ✓

### Food Markets (Hackney/City)

Any grilled chicken, salmon, or fish main with a carb side gets you to 35–45g with no effort. Default to these when available — more protein per pound than Pret, better carb load for afternoon.

### Meal Deal (Sainsbury's/Tesco/M&S)

- Best sandwich choices: chicken club, chicken tikka, egg mayo (+add second egg pot)
- Avoid: cheese ploughman, hummus wraps (protein too low)
- Best sides: boiled egg snack pack, chicken bites, full-fat yoghurt

## Dinner (~35g) — Home Cooked

Simple formula: **150–200g protein source + carb base + vegetables.**

| Protein Source | Serving | Protein |
|---------------|---------|---------|
| Chicken breast | 180g | ~43g |
| Chicken thighs (boneless) | 200g | ~36g |
| Salmon fillet | 200g | ~40g |
| Pork tenderloin | 180g | ~41g |
| Sea bass / cod fillet | 200g | ~36g |
| Lamb mince | 150g | ~30g |
| Eggs (large) | 4 eggs | ~24g |
| Chickpeas + chicken (combo) | 100g + 120g | ~35g |

**Carb base on training days:** rice, pasta, potatoes — aiming for 5–6g carbs/kg bodyweight on Tuesday/Thursday/Sunday.

**Takeaway once a week:** Thai, Japanese, and Indian dishes with chicken or fish are fine. Avoid creamy pasta or pizza nights as a post-training meal — carb/protein ratio works against recovery.

## Key Rules

1. **Don't stress the number daily** — the breakfast alone (~36g) puts you ahead of most people. Focus energy on making lunch protein-forward, not perfect.
2. **Lunch is the main lever.** That's where the gap opens on busy London workdays.
3. **No supplements needed.** The food setup above hits targets without protein shakes. If you're travelling or in a pinch, a protein bar is fine as a bridge.
4. **Carbs matter more than protein on hard training days.** Getting 5–6g/kg carbs on Tuesday/Thursday/Sunday is as important as hitting protein. Underfuelling carbs is the more common mistake.
5. **Don't reduce portions to "eat clean."** You're training at volume. Undereating is the risk, not overeating.
