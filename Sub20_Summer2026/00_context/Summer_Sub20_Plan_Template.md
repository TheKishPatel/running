## Key Sessions Library

### Tempo Run Variations

**Continuous Tempo:**
- 3-6km at threshold pace
- HR 168-180 BPM
- "Hard but sustainable" effort
- Purpose: Lactate threshold development

**Split Tempo:**
- 2×3km or 3×2km at threshold pace
- 2-3 minute jog recovery
- HR 170-180 BPM during reps
- Purpose: Volume at threshold with recovery breaks

**Progressive Tempo:**
- Start at easy pace, progress to threshold
- Example: 6km total - 5:30, 5:00, 4:40, 4:30, 4:20, 4:15/km
- HR building from 155 → 180 BPM
- Purpose: Aerobic power + threshold in single session

**Tempo Finish Long Run:**
- Easy long run with tempo final portion
- Example: 2km easy, 6km steady, 3km tempo, 1km cool
- HR 145-155 during steady, 170-178 during tempo
- Purpose: Threshold work under fatigue

### Interval Session Variations

**Short Intervals (400-600m):**
- Distance: 400m or 600m
- Pace: 3:50-4:00/km (faster than race pace)
- Reps: 6-8 × 400m or 4-6 × 600m
- Recovery: 90 seconds jog
- HR: 180-186+ BPM
- Purpose: Pure speed, turnover, VO2max

**Medium Intervals (800-1200m):**
- Distance: 800m or 1200m
- Pace: 3:58-4:05/km (race pace to slightly slower)
- Reps: 5-6 × 800m or 4-5 × 1200m
- Recovery: 90-120 seconds jog
- HR: 177-182 BPM
- Purpose: VO2max, speed endurance

**Long Intervals (1600-2000m):**
- Distance: 1600m or 2000m
- Pace: 4:00-4:05/km (race pace to slightly slower)
- Reps: 3-4 × 1600m or 2-3 × 2000m
- Recovery: 2-2:30 minutes jog
- HR: 175-180 BPM
- Purpose: Race-specific endurance, mental toughness

**Pyramid Session:**
- Example: 400m, 800m, 1200m, 1600m, 1200m, 800m, 400m
- Pace: 3:55-4:05/km depending on distance
- Recovery: 90-120 seconds jog
- HR: 175-182 BPM
- Purpose: Variety, mental engagement, speed endurance

### Long Run Variations

**Easy Aerobic:**
- 8-12km at easy pace (5:30-5:40/km)
- HR <150 BPM
- Conversational pace
- Purpose: Aerobic base, recovery

**Progressive:**
- Start easy, build to steady/tempo
- Example: 2km @ 6:00, 6km @ 5:40, 3km @ 4:50, 1km @ 6:00
- HR building from 140 → 165 BPM
- Purpose: Aerobic power development

**Sustained Effort:**
- Longer duration at steady pace
- 10-12km at 5:30/km
- HR 145-155 BPM
- Purpose: Aerobic endurance

**Trail Long:**
- 8-12km on trails
- Pace varies with terrain (5:40-6:15/km)
- Norda 001 shoes
- Purpose: Aerobic base + discipline management + reduced impact

## Race Day Strategy

### Pre-Race Routine (2 Hours Before)

**120 minutes before start:**
- Wake up (if early race)
- Light breakfast: Toast + banana + 500ml water
- Begin gentle movement (walking around house)

**90 minutes before:**
- Arrive at race venue
- Bathroom visit
- Collect race number if needed
- Scout the course start/finish

**60 minutes before:**
- Light dynamic stretching (leg swings, lunges, arm circles)
- Sip water (small amounts)
- Mental visualization of race plan

**30 minutes before:**
- Final bathroom visit
- 10 minute easy jog warmup (very relaxed)
- 3-4 × 50m strides (build to 90% speed, not sprinting)
- Back to start area

**15 minutes before:**
- Remove warmup layer
- Final mental check: "Conservative start, build gradually, finish strong"
- Get to start line

**5 minutes before:**
- Light jogging in place
- Deep breathing
- Race mode activated

### Race Pacing Strategy - Conservative (Recommended)

**Target: 19:50 (sub-20 by 10 seconds)**

| Km | Target Pace | Target Time | HR Expected | Effort (1-10) | Mental Cue |
|----|-------------|-------------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| 1 | 4:02/km | 4:02 | 165-170 | 6-7/10 | "Controlled, find rhythm" |
| 2 | 4:00/km | 8:02 | 172-176 | 7/10 | "Settle at race pace" |
| 3 | 3:58/km | 12:00 | 176-180 | 7-8/10 | "Building, feeling strong" |
| 4 | 3:56/km | 15:56 | 178-182 | 8-9/10 | "Push hard, almost there" |
| 5 | 3:54/km | 19:50 | 182-186+ | 9-10/10 | "Empty everything, sprint finish" |

## Contingency Protocols

### Illness During Training

**Symptoms ABOVE neck (runny nose, sneezing, minor sore throat):**
- Day 1: Complete rest
- Day 2: If improving → easy test run 3km @ 6:00/km
- Day 3: If clear → resume easy running
- Day 4-5: Easy running only
- Day 6: Resume quality training if 100% clear

**Symptoms BELOW neck (chest congestion, cough, phlegm):**
- Complete rest until 100% symptom-free
- No testing with runs
- Typical duration: 5-14 days
- Return protocol:
  - Day 1 after clear: Test run 4km @ 6:00/km, HR <145
  - Days 2-4: Easy running 5-8km
  - Day 5: Light tempo 3km @ 4:30/km
  - Day 6: Resume normal training if all sessions feel good

**If illness in final 4 weeks before race:**
- 1-3 days lost: Continue with plan, skip makeup sessions
- 4-7 days lost: Adjust race goal 15-30 seconds slower
- 8-14 days lost: Strongly consider postponing race
- 14+ days lost: Race should be postponed or DNS

### Missed Sessions

**One session missed (any reason):**
- Do NOT make up
- Continue with next scheduled session
- Impact: Minimal

**Two consecutive sessions missed:**
- Resume with easier version of next session
- Example: 6km tempo → 4km tempo
- Get back on track week after

**Full week missed:**
- Resume at -1 phase intensity
- Example: If in Phase 3, resume at Phase 2 intensity
- Rebuild for 1 week, then progress

**Two+ weeks missed:**
- Restart plan from Phase 1
- Reassess race date (likely needs postponing)

### Injury Niggles

**Minor soreness (2-4/10 pain):**
- Continue training if:
  - Pain doesn't increase during run
  - Pain disappears after warmup
  - No limping or form changes
- Monitor carefully
- Ice after sessions
- Consider easy runs on trail (lower impact)

**Moderate pain (5-6/10):**
- Take 2-3 days complete rest
- If improved → test with easy 3km
- If not improved → see physio
- Do NOT train through this

**Significant pain (7+/10):**
- Immediate stop
- Ice, elevate, rest
- See physio within 48 hours
- Likely 1-2 weeks minimum rest

### Overtraining Symptoms

**Early warning signs:**
- Morning HR elevated 5+ BPM for 3+ days
- Persistent fatigue despite adequate sleep
- Mood changes (irritability, low motivation)
- Target paces feeling much harder than expected
- Poor sleep quality

**Response:**
- Take 2-3 days complete rest
- Next session: easy run only (6km @ 6:00/km)
- If feeling better: Resume with -1 intensity
- If still tired: Additional 3-4 days rest
- Consider: More sleep, less life stress, nutrition check

### Race Day Issues

**Feeling terrible on race morning:**
- Assess: Illness? Poor sleep? Overtraining?
- If illness symptoms: DNS (Do Not Start)
- If just nerves/poor sleep: Still race, adjust goal conservatively
- If overtraining: Race easy or DNS

**Stomach issues pre-race:**
- Nothing new race morning
- Bathroom access crucial
- If severe: DNS or accept slower time

**Weather extreme (heat, cold, rain, wind):**
- Heat >25°C: Adjust goal 15-30 seconds slower per degree above 20°C
- Cold <5°C: Extra warmup, adjust first km slower
- Heavy rain: Adjust goal 10-20 seconds slower
- Strong wind: Adjust goal 15-30 seconds slower, use other runners as windbreak

**Pacing disaster (went out too fast):**
- Km 1 at 3:50: SLOW DOWN immediately to 4:05-4:10, assess at 2km
- Km 2 feeling wrecked: Settle at 4:15-4:20, salvage sub-21
- Don't panic, don't quit, adjust goal and finish strong

**Feeling unexpectedly good:**
- Don't get excited and sprint
- Allow pace to build naturally
- Check: If km 3 at 3:55 feels easy (6/10), push km 4-5
- Stay disciplined, execute plan
